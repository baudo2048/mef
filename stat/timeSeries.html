Time Series

Cominciamo da questa considerazione:
In general we consider a time series of observations on some variable, for example, the unemployment rate, denoted as \(Y_1, ..., Y_T\). Quindi \(Y_1\) represents unemployment rate at time 1 and \(Y_T\) represents unemployment rate at time \(T\).
La considerazione precedente continua così:
These observations will be considered realizations of random variables that can be described by some stochastic process.
Ed infine:
Our aim is to describe the properties of this stochastic process by means of a relatively simple model. It will be of particular importance how observations corresponding to different time periods are related, so that we can exploit the dynamic properties of the series to generate predictions for future periods.
