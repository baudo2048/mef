\( \sigma \)-algebra



Definition Let \(X\) be a set. A family \(A\) of subsets of \(X\) is said to be an \(\sigma\)-algebra in \(X\) if \(A\) has the following properties:
  1. \(X \in A\)
  2. Closed under complement
  3. Closed under countably union

Proposition (Intersection of \(\sigma\)-algebras)
The intersection of any \(\sigma\)-algebra of the same \(X\) is a \(\sigma\)-algebra of \(X\).

Proposition (Existence of the smallest \(\sigma\)-algebra)
Let \(X\) be a set.
Let \(\mathcal{F}\) be a family of subsets of \(X\).
There exists a \(\sigma\)-algebra containing all subsets of \(\mathcal{F}\).
