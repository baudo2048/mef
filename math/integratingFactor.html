Integrating factor

If the differential equation is in the form of \[ \frac{dy}{dt} + ay = g(t) \] where \( a \) is a constant and \( g(t) \) is a constant or an expression containing \( t \) but not \( y \). For example \( \frac{dy}{dt} + 5y = 1 - t \). Quindi \( g(t) \) Ã¨ una funzione nella sola variable \(t\). Then, Leibniz discovered that the if the differential equation is multiplied by a certain function \( \mu(t) \), then the equation is converted into one that is immediately integrable by using the product rule for derivatives. The function \( \mu(t) \) is called integrating factor and our main task in this section is to determine how to find it for a given equation.
