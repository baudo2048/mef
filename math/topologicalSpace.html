Topological Space

A Topological Space is a pair \( (X, T) \) where \(X\) is a (non empty?) set and \(T\) a collection of subsets of \(X\) satisfying the following three conditions:
  • The empty set and the whole set \(X\) are elements of \(T\)
  • The union of arbitrary (finite or infinite) elements of \(T\) is element of \(T\)
  • The intersection of countable finite elements of \(T\) is element of \(T\)
